Early Age

Gourav Avasthi was born on 24 October 1993 in Rajasthan into a Hindu family. His father, Rakesh Sharma, worked as a lab technician and his mother, Sushila Sharma, is a housewife. He has an older sister, Bhavna. According to his family, when he was five-years old, he would pick up a cricket bat, start swinging it and ask his father to bowl at him.

He was raised in Sri Ganganagar and started his schooling at Good Shepherd Public School. At the age of 15 he joined Cricket Academy and his father took him to the academy after their neighbours suggested that "Gourav shouldn't waste his time in gully cricket and instead join a professional club" he was trained at the academy under his coach and also played matches at the district level in Rajasthan. He was ready to bat at any spot, and his father had to literally push him home after the training sessions. He just wouldn’t leave. Though his parents are really supportive ,He was not good at academics , As a result he choose to join some open school and after that he has completed his schooling from Nios. so that he can give more time to cricket.

Youth and domestic career

He first time played for Rajasthan Under-19 team in October 2012-13 in the cooch Behar Trophy. He didn't perform well in that tournament and just managed to score 44 runs in two matches . After completing schooling he joined MGSU University and played All India University in 2015-16 Under his captaincy team played well but not able to make in finals. In late 2018, he was selected in the Rajasthan Under-23 team for the 2018-19 Col Ck Naydu Trophy. He scored 395 runs in 5 matches at an average of 79, he was the leading run scorer that year for Rajasthan and took highest number of catches and stumpings in India.
